## 🔑 Load Management Rules  
- **Strain**  
  - Green: <1,800  
  - Amber: 1,800–2,200  
  - Red: >2,200  
- **ACWR**  
  - Green: 0.8–1.3  
  - Amber: 1.3–1.5  
  - Red: >1.5  
- **Monotony**  
  - Green: <1.5  
  - Amber: 1.5–2.0  
  - Red: >2.0  

---

## 🔄 Recovery & Adaptation Rules  
- **Recovery Index**  
  - Green: HRV stable, RestHR steady, Form ≥0  
  - Amber: HRV ↓15–25%, RestHR +5 bpm  
  - Red: HRV ↓>25% or RestHR +10 bpm  
- **Sleep**  
  - Green: >8h avg  
  - Amber: 7–8h avg  
  - Red: <7h avg  
- **Consistency**  
  - Amber: >2 missed sessions/week  
  - Red: >1 missed week in block

## ⚡ Training Quality & Seiler’s Principles  
- **Polarisation Index**  
  - Green: ≥0.8 (≥70% Z1/Z2, ≤20% tempo, ~10% high-intensity)  
  - Amber: 0.6–0.8 (mid-zone creep)  
  - Red: <0.6 (threshold-heavy)  

### 🎯 Session Goals (Seiler’s Framework)  
- Long sessions: 2–6h low-intensity → build durability.  
- Interval sessions: VO₂max/threshold sets (20–40 min work, e.g. 4×8 min @ ~90% HRmax).  
- Tempo/race-pace sessions: sparing use, race-specific sharpening.  
👉 Coaching rule: aim for **Quality Session Balance** = ≥2 quality sessions/week (at least one long, one interval).  

### ⚡ Proven Interval Structures  
- Gold standard: 4×8 min @ ~90% HRmax.  
- Alternatives: 5×6, 3×12, 6×5.  
- Less effective: many threshold “grey zone” sessions.  

### 🔄 Hard Days Hard, Easy Days Easy  
- Easy days: true low-intensity (<20% Z3/Z4).  
- Hard days: meaningful intensity or volume.  
👉 Flag recovery days with >20% Z3/Z4 as violations.  

### 📈 Long-Term Adaptation  
- Consistency > single peaks.  
- CTL ↑ +3–5 per week over months = strong base building.  
- Consistency Index (planned vs completed) is a key predictor.

## 🎯 Periodisation Phases  
- **Build:** CTL ↑ steadily (+3–7/week), ATL <110.  
- **Overload:** ACWR >1.3, Strain 1,900–2,200, HRV dip.  
- **Deload:** Load ↓ ~40%, ATL <80, HRV rebound ≥55 ms.  
- **Consolidation:** CTL stable ±2, HRV steady, VO₂max preserved.  

---

## 🏁 Tapering Guidelines  
- 10–14 days pre-race:  
  - ATL ↓40–60%  
  - Maintain intensity (lower volume only)  
  - HRV trending upward  
- Red flag: ATL not ↓ ≥30% → insufficient taper.  

---

## 🟢 Green Flags  
- CTL >100 sustained = strong aerobic base.  
- VO₂max stable or improving.  
- HRV rebounds post-deload.  
- Polarisation balance (≥70% Z1/Z2).  
- Quality Session Balance achieved.  
- Durability Index (avgDecoupling) ≤5%.  

---

## 🚨 Red & Amber Flags  
- **Subjective vs Objective mismatch**  
  - ⚠️ RPE ≤3 / Feel ≤“moderate” but TSS >150 → under-reporting.  
  - ⚠️ RPE ≥8 / Feel ≥“very hard” but TSS <50 → non-training stress.  
- **Recovery mismatch**  
  - ❌ HRV <35 ms for >5 days.  
  - ❌ RestHR +>10 bpm above baseline.  
- **Quality Session Balance**  
  - ⚠️ Only 1 quality session/week.  
  - ❌ No quality sessions.  
- **Durability Index (avgDecoupling)**  
  - ⚠️ 5–7% drift across endurance sessions.  
  - ❌ >7% drift across endurance sessions.

## 🏅 Sport Profiles  

### Ironman / Long-Course Triathlon  
- Volume: 20–25h typical.  
- Key: long ride + long run bricks.  
- Taper: 2–3 weeks, load ↓50–60%, intensity maintained.  

### Gran Fondo / Road Cycling  
- Volume: 600–900 TSS weekly.  
- Polarisation index >0.9 typical.  
- Taper: 7–10 days, keep threshold intensity.  

### Marathon  
- Key long runs: >30 km.  
- Overload Strain: ~1,800–2,000.  
- Taper: 2–3 weeks, load ↓40–50%.  

### Ultramarathon  
- High volume, monotony risk.  
- HRV & sleep = limiting factors.  
- Taper: 3–4 weeks gradual.  

### Short-Course Tri / Time Trial / 10k  
- Volume lower (8–14h), intensity higher.  
- Polarisation index can drop to 0.7–0.8.  
- Taper: 5–7 days, maintain sharpness.

- PRs: readiness vs historical best.
